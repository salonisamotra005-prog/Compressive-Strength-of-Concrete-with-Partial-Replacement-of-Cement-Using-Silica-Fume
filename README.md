# Compressive Strength of Concrete using Silica Fume Replacement

## Overview
This project studies the effect of partial replacement of cement with silica fume on the compressive strength of M20 grade concrete. Concrete cubes were prepared using a 10% silica fume replacement and tested at 7 days and 28 days to evaluate strength improvement.

## Objective
- To evaluate compressive strength of M20 concrete  
- To study effect of 10% silica fume replacement  
- To compare early and long-term strength  
- To analyze performance improvement  

## Materials Used
- Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC)  
- Silica Fume (10% replacement)  
- Fine Aggregate (Sand)  
- Coarse Aggregate (Crushed stone)  
- Water  

## Mix Proportion
- Grade: M20  
- Mix Ratio: 1 : 1.5 : 3  
- Water-Cement Ratio: 0.50  
- Cube Size: 150 mm × 150 mm × 150 mm  

## Methodology
1. Materials were weighed as per mix design  
2. Dry mixing of cement, silica fume, and sand  
3. Coarse aggregate added and mixed  
4. Water added gradually  
5. Concrete poured into cube moulds  
6. Cubes demoulded after 24 hours  
7. Water curing performed  
8. Compression test conducted at 7 and 28 days  

## Results

| Cube | Age (Days) | Compressive Strength (MPa) |
|------|------------|-----------------------------|
| 1 | 7 | 26.28 |
| 2 | 28 | 45.10 |
| 3 | 28 | 44.20 |

## Key Findings
- 7-day strength reached 26.28 MPa  
- 28-day strength reached about 44–45 MPa  
- Significant strength improvement observed  
- Silica fume enhances concrete density  
- Better long-term performance achieved  

## Conclusion
Partial replacement of cement with 10% silica fume increases compressive strength of M20 concrete. The results show improved long-term strength and better performance compared to conventional concrete.
